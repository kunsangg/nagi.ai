Communication-Efficient Learning of Deep Networks from Decentralized Data
McMahan, Moore, Ramage, Hampson, Arcas · Google · 2017 · Cited 18,000+ times
What they did
Introduced FedAvg — the algorithm that started federated learning as a field. Instead of sending raw patient data to a central server, each hospital trains a local model and only sends model weight updates. The server averages them. Privacy is preserved because raw data never leaves the device.
Why it matters for your topic
This is the origin paper. Every healthcare privacy paper you read will reference this one. Understanding FedAvg first means everything else will click into place. Read this before anything else in your map.
Key terms
FedAvg Decentralized training Model aggregation Gradient updates Non-IID data Communication rounds
Limitations
Assumes all devices participate equally — unrealistic in hospitals with varying infrastructure
Does not address adversarial attacks on gradient updates
Performance drops significantly with highly non-IID data distributions
