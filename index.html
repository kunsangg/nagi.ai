The Solution
01 / Discover

Find the papers that actually matter

Enter any research topic and Nagi builds you a curated map of the 8–12 most important papers in that space. Ranked by relevance. Each one explained in plain language — what it is, why it matters, and whether you should read it first.

No more scrolling through 50,000 results. No more random bookmarking. Just the papers that move the needle.

  • Ranked by relevance to your exact topic
  • Plain language summary for each paper
  • Why each paper matters explained
  • What to read first — and what to skip
  • Updated with recent publications

02 / Understand

Every paper in plain language

Open any paper inside Nagi and get a complete structured breakdown — in language that actually makes sense. No jargon walls. No re-reading the same sentence four times.

You'll know exactly what the researchers did, why it matters, what the limitations are, and how to think about it critically.

  • What they did — methodology simplified
  • Why it matters — real world significance
  • Key terms defined in plain language
  • Limitations and weaknesses surfaced
  • How to cite it correctly

03 / Synthesize

See how it all connects

Research doesn't exist in isolation. Papers build on each other, contradict each other, and fill each other's gaps. Nagi shows you the landscape — who said what, who disagrees, and where the field is heading.

Stop reading papers in isolation. Start understanding the field.

  • Visual map of how papers relate
  • Where researchers agree and disagree
  • Which papers are foundational vs recent
  • How ideas have evolved over time
  • The debates that are still unresolved

04 / Discover gaps

Find what nobody has researched yet

The most valuable thing in research isn't what exists — it's what doesn't. Nagi surfaces the unanswered questions, the understudied areas, and the white spaces in any field.

For students writing proposals. For researchers looking for their next contribution. For anyone who wants to do work that actually adds something new.

  • Unanswered questions surfaced automatically
  • Understudied areas highlighted
  • White spaces in the literature identified
  • Perfect for research proposals
  • Helps you position your own contribution

05 / Write

Go from papers to writing — instantly

Once you understand your field, Nagi helps you structure it. Generate a literature review outline from your paper set — organised by theme, argument, and narrative flow.

Not a summary. A thinking structure you can actually write from. The difference between a shallow lit review and a great one.

  • Outline generated from your paper set
  • Organised by theme and argument
  • Narrative flow built in
  • Gaps and transitions highlighted
  • Export to any format
